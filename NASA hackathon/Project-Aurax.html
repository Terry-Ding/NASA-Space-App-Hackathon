Problem Definition - Mars Waste Management

The Challenge

During a three-year mission, an eight-person crew would accumulate over 12,000 kg of inorganic waste, which poses a significant challenge. If not addressed promptly, this could result in permanent damage to the Martian environment.

Goal

Find an efficient and reliable way to recycle and reuse waste on Mars to create a sustainable closed-loop system.

Objectives

  • Reliability: Operate for years without failure
  • Cost-Effective: Minimize implementation costs
  • Eco-Friendly: Zero harmful emissions
  • Crew-Friendly: Buildable by 8-person crew

Constraints

  • Must be completed within one week
  • No uncontrolled burning on Mars
  • Cost-effective implementation
  • Simple to implement and operate

Proposed Solutions

Rover-Based Collection

Reuse materials to build autonomous rovers for waste collection and Mars exploration.

Habitat Expansion

Use recycled waste to build additional habitats for larger Martian communities.

System Overview

The Challenge: During a hypothetical three-year mission to Mars and back, an eight-person crew would accumulate 12,600 kg of inorganic waste—including packaging materials, textiles, and structural materials. Transporting additional resources from Earth or sending trash back is expensive and inefficient, creating a pressing need for sustainable recycling systems.

Our Solution: The MHRMS transforms Mars habitat waste into valuable manufacturing feedstock, creating a sustainable closed-loop economy that reduces Earth dependency by 60-80% while enabling long-duration missions and habitat expansion.

True Zero-Waste Achievement

Direct Recycling: 96% of input waste is converted into usable filament and manufacturing feedstock.

Residual Processing: The remaining 4% waste (contaminated materials, processing residue) is combined with MGS-1 Martian regolith to create:

  • Structural ceramics
  • Habitat expansion materials
  • Construction aggregates

Result: 100% Material Utilization Rate - Absolutely Zero Waste

Processing Modules

Four specialized modules handle all waste streams, from carbon and polymers to metals and composites, producing high-quality manufacturing feedstock.

Outputs: Carbon-composite filament, activated carbon filters, graphite components, radiation shielding blocks

Capacity: 5-10 kg/day composite filament

Power: 2-3 kW | Components: Ball mill (50kg), compression press (75kg), heating (25kg), mixing chamber (25kg), extrusion die (15kg), controls (10kg)

Module 2: Polymer Recycling Station

240 kg | 5-8 kg/day | 1.5-2.5 kW

Process: Shredding → Drying → Melting → Filtration → Extrusion → Quality Control → Spooling

Outputs: PE, PP, EVA, Polyester, Nylon filaments (±0.05mm tolerance)

Capacity: 5-8 kg/day plastic filament

Power: 1.5-2.5 kW | Components: Shredder w/ HEPA (80kg), heating chamber (60kg), extruder (45kg), sensors (8kg), vapor capture (35kg), spooler (12kg)

Mars Environment Adaptations

Our system leverages Mars' unique conditions as advantages: thin atmosphere prevents oxidation during metal melting, efficient heat rejection in low-pressure environment, and 24.6-hour sols allow extended processing cycles. All modules include thermal management for -153°C to 20°C temperature swings, sealed chambers to capture sublimating volatiles, and dust mitigation systems.
