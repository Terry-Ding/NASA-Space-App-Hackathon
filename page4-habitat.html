Special Controlled Environment Habitat

Mission & Design Philosophy

Mission

  • Dedicated and fully controlled environment
  • Demonstrate autonomous operation with high efficiency, safety, and minimal crew involvement
  • Reuse waste materials on Mars

Goals

  • Zero emission and fully sustainable
  • Maximize utilization of Martian waste
  • Integrated habitat electrical grid powered by renewable or in-situ sources
  • Highly reliable and maintainable with minimal crew intervention

Rover Integration

Fleet Docking Bays Designed With:

  • Automated charging interface
  • Material transfer ports to storage pods
  • Pathways for autonomous navigation within the habitat

Rover Types Supported:

  • Carbon collector
  • Polymer/plastic collector
  • Metal collector

Workflow (Autonomous & Modular)

  1. Material Collection
    Rovers gather materials outside → dock at habitat → deposit into staging pods.
  2. Material Sorting
    Automated conveyors and robotic arms sort materials by category.
    Pre-processing includes grinding, drying, and shredding.
  3. Processing
    Modules 1–3 handle:
    • Carbon processing
    • Polymer/fabric processing
    • Metal processing
    Outputs include filament, power feedstock, and raw materials with integrated quality control.
  4. Manufacturing
    Module 4 3D-prints finished products: tools, replacement parts, and habitat components.
    CNC finishing ensures precision and tolerance adherence.
  5. Rover Maintenance
    Dedicated maintenance/inspection rovers perform minor repairs.
    Automated monitoring systems detect anomalies in real time.
  6. Future Additions
    New processing modules, advanced manufacturing technologies, or expanded storage pods can be seamlessly integrated.
