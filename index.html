System Overview

The Challenge: During a hypothetical three-year mission to Mars and back, an eight-person crew would accumulate 12,600 kg of inorganic waste—including various packaging materials, textiles, and structural materials. This scenario creates a pressing need to recycle available materials, rather than execute the expensive and inefficient processes of transporting additional resources from Earth and/or sending trash back to Earth.

Our Solution: The MHRMS transforms Mars habitat waste into valuable manufacturing feedstock, creating a sustainable closed-loop economy that reduces Earth dependency by 60-80% while enabling long-duration missions and habitat expansion.

Key Innovations

Our system goes beyond NASA's existing Trash-to-Gas approach by producing both propellant gases AND solid manufacturing feedstock. We integrate CO₂ extraction byproducts, regolith processing, and complete 3D printing capabilities to create a true closed-loop manufacturing economy.

Processing Modules

Four specialized modules handle all waste streams, from carbon and polymers to metals and composites, producing high-quality manufacturing feedstock.

▶

Module 1: Carbon Processing Station

Total Mass: 200 kg | Power: 2-3 kW Required / 3 kW Maximum

Converts CO₂ extraction byproducts, activated carbon, and organic waste into composite filament and radiation shielding materials.

Process: Grinding → Mixing with polymer binder → Extrusion → Spooling

Outputs: Carbon-composite filament, activated carbon filters, graphite components, radiation shielding blocks

Capacity: 5-10 kg/day composite filament

Components: Ball mill (50kg), compression press (75kg), heating (25kg), mixing chamber (25kg), extrusion die (15kg), controls (10kg)

▶

Module 2: Polymer Recycling Station

Total Mass: 240 kg | Power: 1.5-2.5 kW Required / 2.5 kW Maximum

Processes plastics, fabrics, EVA waste, and packaging into precision 3D printing filaments with zero microplastic release.

Process: Shredding → Drying → Melting → Filtration → Extrusion → Quality Control → Spooling

Outputs: PE, PP, EVA, Polyester, Nylon filaments (±0.05mm tolerance)

Capacity: 5-8 kg/day plastic filament

Components: Shredder w/ HEPA (80kg), heating chamber (60kg), extruder (45kg), sensors (8kg), vapor capture (35kg), spooler (12kg)

▶

Module 3: Metal Processing Station

Total Mass: 380 kg | Power: 3-7 kW Required / 7 kW Maximum

Recycles metal waste from tools, structures, and electronics into wire, powder, and cast components.

Process: Shredding → Melting → Casting → Wire Drawing → Quality Control

Outputs: Aluminum, steel, copper wire (1.75-2.85mm), metal powders, cast parts

Capacity: 3-5 kg/day metal products

Components: Metal shredder (100kg), induction furnace (150kg), wire dies (40kg), casting molds (30kg), cooling system (25kg), atomizer optional (35kg)

▶

Module 4: Integrated Manufacturing Hub

Total Mass: 225 kg | Power: 0.5-2 kW Required / 2 kW Maximum

3D prints finished products using feedstock from Modules 1-3, with CNC finishing for precision parts.

Capabilities: FDM printing (plastic/composite/metal), direct pellet extrusion, CNC machining, post-processing

Outputs: Tools, utensils, storage containers, habitat components, life support parts, scientific instruments

Capacity: 0.5-2 kg/day finished parts

Components: FDM plastic printer (35kg), metal FDM printer (55kg), pellet extruder (40kg), CNC station (60kg), testing equipment (20kg), post-processing (15kg)

Total MHRMS Processing Equipment Mass: 1,045 kg (all modules combined) | Daily Combined Output: 13.5-25 kg processed materials

4. Composite Rover

Fleet Integration & Workflow

Storage/Transport rovers deliver materials to correct MHRMS module inputs with sealed, contamination-free transfer

3. Processing Phase

MHRMS modules handle recycling, filament production, and manufacturing while rovers continue collection operations

4. Maintenance Phase

Maintenance rover monitors system health, performs repairs, and ensures continuous operation with minimal crew involvement

Mars Environment Adaptations

Our system leverages Mars' unique conditions as advantages: thin atmosphere prevents oxidation during metal melting, efficient heat rejection in low-pressure environment, and 24.6-hour sols allow extended processing cycles. All modules include thermal management for -153°C to 20°C temperature swings, sealed chambers to capture sublimating volatiles, and dust mitigation systems.

🔬 Methodological Notes & Assumptions

Design Approach: All system specifications represent conservative engineering estimates based on current terrestrial technology adapted for Mars conditions (0.38g gravity, 0.6 kPa atmospheric pressure, -153°C to 20°C temperature range).

Key Assumptions:

  • Waste Generation: 12,600 kg over 3-year mission (8-person crew, 1,095 days) based on ISS empirical data and NASA DRA 5.0 scaling
  • Launch Costs: $10,000/kg baseline commonly used in mission planning studies and challenges; current SpaceX Starship projections approximately $100,000/kg to Mars surface
  • Processing Efficiency: 70-85% operational efficiency accounting for Mars environmental challenges, maintenance cycles, and crew workload
  • Energy Consumption: Includes thermal management overhead for Mars temperature extremes
  • Material Recovery: 96% direct recycling rate; remaining 4% (contaminated materials, mixed composites, processing residue) combined with MGS-1 Martian regolith for construction materials

Result: 100% total material utilization with zero waste discharge to Martian environment.
